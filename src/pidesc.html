Pi (Ï€) is usually defined as the ratio of any circle's circumference to its diameter (C/d)

The ratio is the exact same for all circles, even if the sizes are different.

It is an irrational number, meaning that it can't be written as the ratio of two integers

(numbers that aren't fractions). Although pi's digits seem to have no pattern and have passed tests

for normality (each number being equally likely to appear), this has not been proven.
